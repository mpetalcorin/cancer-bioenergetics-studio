# Cancer Bioenergetics StudioA responsive research dashboard for exploring **cancer bioenergetics**, **metabolic plasticity**, **glucose–ketone dynamics**, and **phenotype-dependent intervention responses** using transparent synthetic datasets.The application provides an interactive interface for examining how tumour cells may distribute metabolic activity across glycolysis, mitochondrial oxidative phosphorylation, glutamine metabolism, and fatty-acid oxidation. It also includes a glucose–ketone index calculator and a research-oriented intervention simulator.> **Important:** This application is an educational and computational research prototype. It is not a medical device and must not be used to diagnose, prevent, monitor, or treat cancer.---## OverviewCancer cells do not occupy a single universal metabolic state. Although many tumours display increased glucose uptake and lactate production, substantial subsets retain active mitochondrial respiration and can switch among multiple energy and biosynthetic pathways.Cancer Bioenergetics Studio represents this heterogeneity through four illustrative metabolic phenotypes:- **Glycolytic dominant**- **Oxidative dominant**- **Hybrid high-energy**- **Glutamine-supported**The app integrates these phenotypes with synthetic measurements of glucose uptake, lactate production, oxygen-consumption rate, glutamine use, fatty-acid oxidation, proliferation, glucose, ketones, and simulated treatment response.---## Key Features### Research overview dashboardThe landing page summarises the synthetic analytical framework, including:- 630 synthetic cellular models- Seven biological contexts- Four metabolic phenotypes- 13,440 longitudinal host observations- Eight simulated intervention conditions### Metabolic phenotype explorerInteractively compare relative activity across:- Glycolysis- Oxidative phosphorylation- Glutamine metabolism- Fatty-acid oxidationEach phenotype includes illustrative lactate and basal oxygen-consumption measurements.### Synthetic tumour bioenergetics atlasExplore differences among:- Glioblastoma- Triple-negative breast cancer- Colorectal cancer- Pancreatic cancer- Melanoma- Oxidative lymphoma- Normal epithelial cellsThe atlas illustrates that high lactate production and substantial mitochondrial respiration can coexist.### Glucose–ketone index calculatorThe glucose–ketone index is calculated as:\[\mathrm{GKI} =\frac{\mathrm{glucose\;(mmol/L)}}{\beta\mathrm{-hydroxybutyrate\;(mmol/L)}}\]For glucose measured in mg/dL:\[\mathrm{glucose\;(mmol/L)} =\frac{\mathrm{glucose\;(mg/dL)}}{18}\]The calculator accepts glucose in either mg/dL or mmol/L.> GKI is a dynamic systemic ratio. It is not a direct measurement of tumour metabolism, mitochondrial integrity, tumour burden, or treatment response.### Longitudinal metabolic scenariosVisualise twelve-week synthetic GKI trajectories under four illustrative exposure patterns:- Western-pattern diet- Mediterranean-pattern diet- Low-carbohydrate diet- Ketogenic dietThese curves represent simulated physiological scenarios and not clinical trial outcomes.### Intervention-response simulatorExplore synthetic tumour-cell viability under combinations of:- Reduced carbohydrate availability- Glutaminase inhibition- Mitochondrial complex I inhibitionThe simulator allows users to vary baseline metabolic phenotype and pathway pressure.### Evidence and limitations panelThe app separates:- Established cancer-metabolism principles- Investigational concepts- Claims not demonstrated by the simulation- Important clinical and scientific limitations---## Scientific RationaleCancer metabolism is characterised by heterogeneity and plasticity. Glycolysis, mitochondrial respiration, glutaminolysis, and fatty-acid oxidation can each contribute to tumour growth, survival, redox balance, and biosynthesis.The application therefore avoids assuming that:- all tumour mitochondria are irreversibly defective;- every cancer depends exclusively on glucose;- all cancers are unable to oxidise fatty acids or ketone bodies;- lowering systemic glucose or GKI is sufficient to treat cancer;- one metabolic intervention will be effective across all tumour types.Instead, the app illustrates why metabolic profiling and biomarker-guided combination strategies may be more rational than universal single-pathway targeting.---## Technology Stack- React- TypeScript- Vite- Recharts- Lucide React- CSS- were created to reproduce biologically plausible relationships among:- glucose uptake;- lactate production;- oxygen-consumption rate;- glutamine dependence;- fatty-acid oxidation;- mitochondrial activity;- proliferation;- glucose and ketone concentrations;- metabolic intervention response.The synthetic values are intended for demonstration, software development, teaching, visualisation, and hypothesis generation.They must not be interpreted as:- patient-level observations;- clinical trial results;- treatment-effect estimates;- validated prognostic thresholds;- clinical recommendations.---## Interpretation of the Metabolic Phenotypes### Glycolytic dominantCharacterised by high glucose uptake and lactate production, with comparatively lower respiratory dependence.### Oxidative dominantCharacterised by substantial mitochondrial respiration, spare respiratory capacity, and fatty-acid oxidation.### Hybrid high-energyCharacterised by simultaneous glycolysis and oxidative phosphorylation. This phenotype illustrates metabolic flexibility and potential resistance to single-pathway inhibition.### Glutamine-supportedCharacterised by strong glutamine-dependent anaplerosis and biosynthesis, with context-dependent glycolytic and mitochondrial support.These phenotypes are conceptual categories. Real tumours may occupy intermediate states and change phenotype over time.---## Clinical and Safety LimitationsThis application does not provide medical advice.Do not use the app to:- diagnose cancer;- estimate individual cancer risk;- select cancer treatment;- discontinue or modify prescribed therapy;- prescribe fasting or ketogenic diets;- interpret glucose or ketone measurements without clinical context;- determine whether a tumour will respond to metabolic treatment.Dietary intervention in cancer may carry important risks, including:- loss of body weight;- sarcopenia;- cachexia;- hypoglycaemia;- dehydration;- electrolyte disturbance;- micronutrient deficiency;- dyslipidaemia;- interactions with anticancer treatment.Any dietary or metabolic intervention in a patient with cancer requires supervision by qualified oncology and nutrition professionals.---## Research ExtensionsPotential future developments include:- importing real static synthetic data stored in:```textsrc/data/simulation.ts```The values can be replaced with:- experimentally measured cell-line data;- organoid measurements;- animal-model data;- public cancer datasets;- clinical metabolic observations.When replacing the synthetic dataset, maintain:- documented data provenance;- standardised units;- missing-data handling;- quality-control procedures;- version control;- explicit clinical and statistical limitations.---## Scientific ReferencesSelected peer-reviewed references relevant to the conceptual framework include:-
